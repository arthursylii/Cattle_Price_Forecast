Note: directional accuracy is a weaker signal here than usual — live cattle has been in a near-monotonic 2-year uptrend, so there's little "down" movement in the training data for the model to learn from. MAE/MAPE are the more meaningful accuracy metrics for this series.

On ML vs. Chain divergence: months 1-5 blend the ML forecast with the same real CME futures curve the chain (S/D) model uses, so they track closely there. Beyond month 5, futures coverage runs out and both models fall back to pure long-run assumptions — ML extrapolates the historical price trend, while the chain model uses supply/demand fundamentals (COF, heifer retention, seasonal). Some disagreement between those two independent approaches at the 6-12 month horizon is expected and honest, not a bug — treat convergence in the near months as higher-confidence, and growing divergence further out as a signal that the two methodologies see different things, worth your own judgment call rather than mechanically averaging them.
